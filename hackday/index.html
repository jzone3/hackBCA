What is Local Hack Day?

It's a precursor to hackBCA II that allows students to see what a hackathon is really like without making the full 24 hour commitment. Participants will have the opportunity to attend various workshops for beginner languages and apply the knowledge learned right away during the hackathon. It also provides an environment for seasoned hackers to work on new projects or continue an existing project they've worked on before. Free lunch, dinner, and swag will be distributed throughout the event. It's an awesome opportunity and you should definitely come! See you there.

What if I don't know how to program?

That is totally fine. The whole point of local hack day is to provide newcomers an opportunity to learn about programming and computer science. We will provide various workshops for people to attend. By the end of the event, you'll know how to program!

Hack? I don't want to get in trouble!

Don't worry! We won't be breaking into a secret headquarters and uploading some malicious software onto their systems. In fact, we'll basically be doing the opposite. The whole point of "hacking" is to make something awesome, new, and to have fun doing it! We're going to help you hack something cool into existence, not out of it.

What can I do here?

There's almost no limit on what you can do! Do you have an idea that crossed your mind in the past? Code it! Do you have a previous project that you want to work on? Continue it! Do you want to learn something new from our workshops? Learn it! There's going to be so much here, we're sure that you won't be at a loss.

Where and when is this exactly?

This is right at the Bergen County Academies in Hackensack, New Jersey. (200 Hackensack Avenue, Hackensack, NJ 07601). The event will run for 12 hours, from 9 AM to 9 PM

What gets provided?

Everything you need to enjoy yourself and be productive will be provided. Snacks, lunch, dinner, drinks, etc. is all included for free. On top of that, we will be giving out swag (a.k.a. free stuff) to everyone that attends! We do not provide computers or hardware to hack on, though. If you're planning on hacking on something unique, you'll have to bring it in yourself!

Workshops

We're going to be hosting a bunch of these throughout the entire event! What are they, you ask? Well, at these 30-45 minute sessions, we will bring in talented individuals and professionals to teach you about something new. Whether it is a language you've yet to learn, or an extremely useful library or software that you never imagined existed, these workshops are intended for you to learn all about them. They're quick, helpful, and to-the-point, so that you won't lose much time attending them and so that you will be able to apply what you've learned right away.
